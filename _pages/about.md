<div class="home-section home-about">

<p>
Hi, I'm Ying-Chun Lee, and you can also call me Thomas. I'm a master's student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the <strong>University of Washington</strong>. I build robot-learning and software systems around Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models, especially the parts that connect policies to real hardware: data collection, teleoperation, deployment, reward modeling, and evaluation.
</p>

<p>
My robotics experience spans a Robotics Engineer Internship at <strong>Chang Chun Group</strong>'s Information Center, where I built an Isaac Sim digital twin and imitation-learning platform for the ALOHA VX300S arm; <strong>Husky Robotics</strong> rover perception with OWL-ViT and RealSense depth sensing; and research collaboration with <a href="https://duanjiafei.com/">Jiafei Duan</a> and collaborators from <strong><a href="https://prior.allenai.org/">Ai2 PRIOR</a></strong> on real-robot policy infrastructure.
</p>

<p>
Before UW, I conducted research on deep learning-based shoulder ultrasound segmentation with guidance from <a href="https://experts.illinois.edu/en/persons/yih-kuen-jan/">Prof. Yih-Kuen Jan</a> at the <strong>DRES Lab, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (UIUC)</strong>, and <a href="https://scholars.ncu.edu.tw/en/persons/chih-yang-lin/">Prof. Chih-Yang Lin</a>. That project became a first-author paper in <a href="https://doi.org/10.1109/ACCESS.2024.3432691"><strong>IEEE Access</strong></a>. In June 2026, I'll join <strong>Amazon</strong> as a Software Development Engineer Intern.
</p>
</div>
